The Ministry

Five arms, one calling.

DAAM carries the Gospel through five connected expressions of service — each one an outlet for a single, unchanging conviction: that the Word of God is meant to be lived, not just heard.

Every arm of DAAM grows from the same root — a conviction that the Gospel is whole, and that ministry should reach the mind, the body, and the spirit alike.

01

Gospel Outreaches Arm

The evangelistic heartbeat of the ministry — crusades, missions, and outreach programmes that carry the Gospel directly into communities, campuses, and nations that have not yet heard it clearly. This arm exists to make sure the message never stays confined to a building.

02

Medical Arm

Free medical outreaches, health education, and clinical support carried out in the same spirit as the rest of the ministry. Drawing directly on Dr. Achimugu's training as a physician and public health specialist, this arm treats healthcare as an act of ministry, not a separate pursuit.

03

Academic Arm

Mentorship, discipleship, and leadership development for students and young scholars. Rooted in Dr. Achimugu's own journey through campus ministry and academic life, this arm exists to raise Christian leaders inside the classroom and the lecture hall, not just the church.

04

Hospitality Arm

The practical, often unseen service of welcoming and caring for guests, visiting ministers, and members of the church community. This arm carries out the quiet conviction that how people are received says as much about the Gospel as what is preached.

05

Publications Arm

The written extension of the ministry's teaching — books, articles, and resources designed to carry the Word to readers a single sermon could never reach. This arm holds Dr. Achimugu's growing body of published work.
